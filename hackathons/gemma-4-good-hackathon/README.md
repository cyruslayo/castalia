**Core Themes:** Health & Sciences, Global Resilience, Future of Education, Digital Equity, Safety & Trust.

## 🚀 Top 10 High-Impact Proposed Projects

### 1. DisasterResp-Agent (Global Resilience + llama.cpp Track)
- **Experts:** Dr. Ada (Agents) & Dr. Sys-O (Systems)
- **Concept:** A swarm of edge agents deployed on battery-powered micro-devices (Raspberry Pi/similar) in disaster zones where internet is down. Utilizing `llama.cpp` and native function calling, they coordinate rescue logistics, map local resources, and communicate via offline mesh networks.
- **Why it Wins:** Incredible storytelling regarding disaster recovery, maximum impact for offline resilience, and high technical depth in edge execution.

### 2. VisionMed Triage (Health & Sciences + LiteRT & Cactus Tracks)
- **Experts:** Dr. Mia (Multimodal) & Dr. Sys-O (Systems)
- **Concept:** A local-first Android mobile application for rural health workers. Using Gemma 4's multimodal capabilities via Google AI Edge's LiteRT, workers photograph skin conditions, wounds, or environmental hazards to receive immediate offline triage protocols.
- **Why it Wins:** directly addresses the "access to care" impact, specifically targets multiple technical tracks (LiteRT, Cactus).

### 3. EduTutor-Unsloth (Future of Education + Unsloth Track)
- **Expert:** Dr. Finn (Fine-Tuning)
- **Concept:** A deeply specialized educational copilot fine-tuned using Unsloth on pedagogies for neurodivergent learning (ADHD, dyslexia, autism). It dynamically shifts its teaching modality and pacing to match the emotional state of the student.
- **Why it Wins:** Deep technical execution in fine-tuning, highly compelling and emotional storytelling about empowering children.

### 4. TruthGuard Local (Safety & Trust + Ollama Track)
- **Experts:** Dr. Eva (Evals) & Dr. Sys-O (Systems)
- **Concept:** A fact-checking companion operating entirely locally via Ollama. It analyzes news articles and social feeds against an indexed offline database to highlight misinformation during pivotal global events without ever sending user browsing data to the cloud.
- **Why it Wins:** Privacy-by-design meets Safety & Trust, strong storytelling addressing modern misinformation.

### 5. LinguaBridge RAG (Digital Equity & Inclusivity)
- **Experts:** Dr. Rachel (RAG) & Dr. Prompto (Prompt Engineering)
- **Concept:** An NGO-focused RAG environment that indexes local legal, immigration, and medical documentation. Utilizing precise prompt engineering, it allows non-native speakers or individuals with lower literacy to orally navigate complex bureaucratic forms in their localized dialects.
- **Why it Wins:** Breaks down "linguistic diversity" barriers as explicitly requested in the prompt, high social impact.

### 6. FarmSense Multimodal (Global Resilience + Cactus Track)
- **Expert:** Dr. Mia (Multimodal)
- **Concept:** A mobile-native wearable/smartphone application for subsistence farmers in the Global South. By snapping an image of a blighted crop, the multimodal model provides immediate, agriculturally sound advice for preventing crop failure, factoring in offline-available local weather data.
- **Why it Wins:** Visually impressive multimodal execution with profound implications for global food security.

### 7. CodeEquity Copilot (Digital Equity & Inclusivity)
- **Experts:** Dr. Ada (Agents) & Dr. Prompto (Prompt Engineering)
- **Concept:** An agentic coding assistant designed specifically to close the AI Skills Gap. Instead of standard corporate documentation, it relates complex programming structures to localized cultural analogies and native language concepts for new developers in developing nations.
- **Why it Wins:** Focuses on the "empowerment and accessibility" requirement, demonstrating a highly tailored use of system prompts and agents.

### 8. MedVoice RAG (Health & Sciences)
- **Expert:** Dr. Rachel (RAG)
- **Concept:** A privacy-compliant local system that vectorizes an entire elderly patient's offline medical history. It acts as an answering engine, translating frightening or complex medical jargon from test results into simple, reassuring, and actionable instructions for the patient and their family.
- **Why it Wins:** Combines human empathy with rigorous localized data retrieval, excellent storytelling angle.

### 9. EdgeClassroom (Future of Education + llama.cpp Track)
- **Experts:** Dr. Sys-O (Systems) & Dr. Finn (Fine-Tuning)
- **Concept:** An offline "server in a box" for remote, off-grid schools. It hosts a quantized Gemma 4 model that serves as a multi-tool agent for teachers—generating quizzes, grading handwritten papers via multimodal capabilities, and tracking student trends without needing internet access.
- **Why it Wins:** Demonstrates AI value where conventional cloud solutions fail, massive storytelling potential.

### 10. Explainable Audit Engine (Safety & Trust)
- **Expert:** Dr. Eva (Evals)
- **Concept:** Utilizing the 26B/31B model to automatically audit and evaluate smaller models deployed in sensitive high-risk environments (e.g., automated hiring, loan approval). It generates human-readable compliance and logic graphs to ensure AI remains "grounded and explainable."
- **Why it Wins:** Directly fulfills the safety requirement for transparency; technically deep use case reflecting enterprise-grade evaluation workflows.
